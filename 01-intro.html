Part 1: Introduction

What this project is about and how we are going to conquer it

Early thoughts

I have always been interested in sports, data, and those simulation games putting both together. Behind the intensity, the competitiveness, the joy of triumph, data play an essential role in shaping the modern boundary of sports. Just like other industries, we use data as reflections and predictions at the same time. What fascinates me the most is that data provide a new perspective to appreciate games. For example, there might be some memorable moments when talking about a particular player's career, but collectively, is our overall impression about a player accurate? We might find ourselves in a position close to ignorance.

So in the portfolio, I believe my topic selection is more like a loose collection of sports analytics applications. Because the data sources vary immensely, I probably will not give out a profound conclusion like "how data would change the sports industry" or "what are the golden standards of being a basketball G.O.A.T. (the Greatest Of All Time)." Instead, this is more or less like a showcase of what can be done when a fan like me stand at the crossroad of sports and data.

The sport I'm picking is basketball because I've been watching games for a while, witnessed the birth of a champion team, the Toronto Raptors (back when they struggled a lot). And comparatively speaking, basketball has the most accessible open data.

An ever-updating roaster of questions

Ok, folks. Let's talk about the game plan.

I'll try to keep the questions consistent this semester, but I definitely cannot guarantee that! The coverage of these questions ranges from tactics, performance to some off-court factors like social network interactions, etc.

Some rough plans about how to digest and answer these questions will be listed below as well.

Q1 Expected points

How to quantify the expected points from one shot?

Inspired by the expected goal metric in soccer, can we apply that in basketball and make it more sensible than the shot percentage?

Q2 Expected threats

Based on "expected points," can we quantify a player's offensive contribution with a dynamic "expected threat" concept?

Or how to quantify such metric when the player is not taking the shot, a.k.a. playing off-ball?

Q3 Clutch moment

When the clock is ticking, who is your most trustworthy ball handler, and who takes the last shot?

Q4 Player interactions

How do players interact with each other off-court?

Do teammates/ex-teammates interact the most?

Q5 Social life vs. game plays

What's the relation (if any) between players' social media exposure and game performances?

The more a player enjoys life, the worse he plays the next day or vice versa? This one is heavily influenced by an old Reddit post1 illustrating James Harden's game night performances vs. the overall strip club rating of the city he plays against that night.

Q6 Off-season topics

What are basketball fans' favorite topics in the off-season?

Another social network-related question to raise.

Q7 Myths of power ranking

How to present teams' power ranking to a casual sports fan?

ELO is an incredible invention, but is it good enough?

Q8 King of the off-season

Who's the king of the off-season?

Another off-season secret. Some players choose to enjoy their lives, hanging out with friends, families; some go overseas for commercial reasons. But there can be only one king to rule it all.

Q9 Sixth man

Sixth man, or a hidden blade?

The league always favors the non-starter with the most ppg in a Sixth Man voting. It sounds like a "Best Bench Offensive Player Award" instead.

Q10 Board man vs max contract

Board man gets paid? Is that true?

I can't help telling you how much I love this one. It's a catchphrase from 2019 NBA champion Kawhi Leonard when he grabs a rebound. But did all those rebounding leaders get paid in the following season?

Q11 Taco Tuesday

Does Lebron James revitalize the term Taco Tuesday?

The 36-year-old Los Angeles Laker star has been famous for sharing his weekly taco dinners on social media since 2019. But does that matter? Does the former cause the latter?

Q12 Unlimited range

Who is the deadliest sniper?

Let us go back to a genuine basketball question. I'm not interested in who is the best 3-pt shooter in the league or who is the most stable catch-and-shoot player waiting in the corner. The real question is, who is the mad man from downtown? Dame Dollar? Chef Curry? Imagine if NBA introduces the rule of a 4-point play. What's going to happen then?

via Gfycat

As a grass-roots, homebrew, amateur sports analyst, I present these questions to tackle, and hopefully, I can give some insights into these.
